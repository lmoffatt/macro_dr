# Figure 1 caption

**Figure 1. One filter step along the interval-likelihood ladder.**

The current from a patch of N_ch = 20 identical two-state (closed ⇌ open) channels is recorded as a time-average over successive measurement intervals, with added instrumental noise. Each column is one Gaussian (moment-closure) approximation to the likelihood of that recording. All four are recursive, meaning the estimate from one interval is carried into the next as its prior, so the recursion axis is held fixed and the columns differ only in what the likelihood conditions the interval-averaged conductance on. R (Recursive) uses the channel conductance at a single instant and ignores the averaging altogether. MR (Mean Recursive) uses the conductance averaged over the interval, conditioned on the state at the interval's start, and predicts its spread with the total per-start-state conductance variance. VR (Variance Recursive) keeps MR's predicted mean and its update, and replaces that total variance with the residual one, the part that remains after conditioning on where the interval ends. IR (Interval Recursive) conditions on the state at both interval endpoints, which lets the covariance between them enter the update. Reading left to right, each column adds one piece of interval structure to the one before it. Two consecutive measurement windows are shown (6 to 10 ms) so that the per-interval operations are visible; the first ten milliseconds of the twelve-millisecond recording are given as a figure supplement.

Rows follow one turn of the filter cycle. (A) Prior: the open probability P_open predicted by the Markov step, before the current of the interval is measured. (B) Observation: the predicted current and its spread (orange), the measured current, and the innovation (blue), which is the measured minus the predicted current. The spread is drawn as an error bar for R, which predicts an instantaneous conductance, and as a band for the three approximations that average over the interval, so the averaging distinction reads directly off the observation row. VR's band is the narrower one wherever the channels are gating, because it carries only the residual variance. (C) Posterior: P_open after the Bayes update. (D) The per-step log-likelihood accumulated over the window, Σ logL, reset to zero at the window start so the four columns begin together.

Colour encodes the filter phase and is shared across the rows. Green is the channel current, the resolved simulated current, drawn in A and C as the realised open fraction (channel current divided by N_ch), the ground truth the estimate tracks. Orange is the predict phase: the Markov step, the prior, and the predicted current. Blue is the observation and the innovation. Purple is the Bayes update and the posterior. Black is the log-likelihood. Arrows mark operations. A state carried in from the neighbouring step is drawn hollow (points) or dashed (segments) to set it apart from the state computed within the panel.

The two non-recursive members of the family, NR (Non-Recursive) and MNR (Mean Non-Recursive), are named in the Theory section to locate this ladder within the wider family, and are not measured here. A version of this figure that includes them is available as a source figure (`figure_1_all.Rmd`).
