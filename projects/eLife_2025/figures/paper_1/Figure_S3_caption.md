# Figure S3 caption

**Figure S3. The full correlation structure and the overconfidence of the four approximations other than IR.**

Companion to Figure 2B, which shows the correlation corner of IR. Here the same corner is drawn for the other four approximations: (A) NR (Non-Recursive), (B) MNR (Mean Non-Recursive), (C) R (Recursive) and (D) MR (Mean Recursive). Each corner is the full lower triangle of the six parameter pairs among the four parameters (log10 k_on, log10 k_off, log10 i, log10 N_ch), on the same recording cell and with the same estimation as Figure 2. Every cell carries three 95% ellipses (the empirical covariance of the cloud in grey, the covariance the approximation reports from its Fisher information in orange dashed, and the distortion-corrected sandwich covariance in blue dotted), the two ellipse-area ratios (empirical over Fisher in orange, the overconfidence factor; empirical over corrected in blue), and three centre markers (the truth as a cross, the empirical mean of the cloud as a filled circle, and the truth plus the bias predicted by the distortion theory as an open circle).

The Fisher information is overconfident for the non-recursive pair (empirical over Fisher of about 10 to 16 for NR and MNR) and mildly so for the recursive pair (about 1.2 to 1.4 for R, 1.5 to 2.1 for MR), while the corrected ratio reads about 1 in every cell of all four corners: the sandwich correction recovers the true spread for every approximation, as it does for IR. R and MR also carry a real bias in the amplitude pair (log10 N_ch, log10 i): the truth cross sits off the cloud, and the open circle (truth plus predicted bias) lands on the filled circle (the empirical mean), so the distortion theory accounts for that bias. In each cell the two ratio numbers are placed in the panel corner that the tilt of the cloud leaves empty.
