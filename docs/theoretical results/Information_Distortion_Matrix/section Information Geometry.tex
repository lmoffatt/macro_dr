Let the total log-likelihood of a trajectory of length $T$ be
\begin{equation}
\ell(\theta) = \sum_{t=1}^T \ell_t(\theta),
\qquad
S(\theta) = \nabla_\theta \ell(\theta) = \sum_{t=1}^T s_t(\theta),
\end{equation}
where $s_t(\theta) = \nabla_\theta \ell_t(\theta)$ is the score contribution of measurement $t$.

\section*{Gaussian Fisher Information (GFI)}

The Gaussian Fisher Information is defined as the curvature of the total predictive log-likelihood,
\begin{equation}
\mathbf H(\theta) := -\mathbb{E}\left[ \nabla_\theta^2 \ell(\theta) \right].
\end{equation}

In our implementation, $\mathbf H$ is computed from the Gaussian predictive model
$y_t \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_t(\theta), \sigma_t^2(\theta))$,
where $(\mu_t,\sigma_t^2)$ are obtained recursively from the filtering procedure.
The matrix $\mathbf H$ therefore defines the local quadratic geometry implied by the Gaussian predictive model.

\section*{Score Covariance Matrix (SCM)}

The Score Covariance Matrix is defined as
\begin{equation}
\mathbf J(\theta) := \mathrm{Cov}\!\left( S(\theta) \right).
\end{equation}

This matrix measures the fluctuation geometry of the total score process,
including all temporal correlations and deviations from Gaussianity.

\section*{Information Distortion Matrix (IDM)}

We define the Information Distortion Matrix as
\begin{equation}
\mathbf C(\theta)
=
\mathbf H(\theta)^{-1/2}
\mathbf J(\theta)
\mathbf H(\theta)^{-1/2}.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
If the Gaussian predictive model fully captures the conditional dynamics,
the score increments behave as independent innovations and
\begin{equation}
\mathbf J(\theta_0) = \mathbf H(\theta_0),
\qquad
\mathbf C(\theta_0) = \mathbf I.
\end{equation}

Eigenvalues $\lambda_i$ of $\mathbf C$ represent direction-dependent variance inflation
(or deflation) relative to the Gaussian Fisher geometry.
Off-diagonal structure reflects parameter coupling not predicted by curvature.
