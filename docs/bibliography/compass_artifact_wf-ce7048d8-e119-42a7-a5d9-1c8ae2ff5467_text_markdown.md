# Novelty assessment of the differentiable symmetric-subspace propagator bundle

**Bottom line up front.** Every individual ingredient of the proposed algorithm has prior art, but the specific combination — (i) symmetric-subspace multinomial state space for N independent identical CTMCs, (ii) binary-doubling-on-N construction of the propagator via multinomial convolution, (iii) the boundary-conditioned **(m, μ, σ²)** bundle for an integrated observable carried through the recursion, and (iv) forward-mode autodiff for gradient-based MLE / Fisher information / HMC — does not appear in any of the six literatures surveyed. The closest single piece of prior art is **Rupp, Schill, Süskind, Lange et al. (2024) "Differentiated uniformization"** (Comput. Stat. 39:3643–3663, DOI 10.1007/s00180-024-01454-9), which solves the same inference problem class — CTMC ensembles whose Q is a tensor sum, ∂exp(tQ)/∂θ for HMC on stochastic SIR — but via differentiated uniformization rather than binary doubling, and produces only m_ij and its derivative (no μ, σ²). The Kronecker-sum / multinomial-convolution structure itself is folklore in second quantization (Negele–Orland; Doi–Peliti; Prosen's third quantization for quadratic Lindbladians) and was made probabilistically explicit for monomolecular CMEs by **Jahnke & Huisinga (2007)**. The endpoint-conditioned (μ, σ²) of integrated functionals is standard at the single-CTMC level in molecular evolution (**Hobolth–Jensen 2011**, **Minin–Suchard 2008**, **Tataru–Hobolth 2011**). What is genuinely new is welding these together at the population level with autodiff in mind.

## 1. Ion channel / patch-clamp literature

The Colquhoun–Hawkes corpus established the Q-matrix machinery and the conditional moments of integrated quantities (mean and variance of total open time within a burst) at the **single-channel** level; no paper in this field constructs the (m, μ, σ²) bundle for an N-channel ensemble by binary doubling on N, and no paper does autodiff-through-the-propagator at N>1. Independence/Kronecker-sum structure for N-channel patches is mathematical folklore in the field — implicit in every macroscopic-fluctuation paper since the 1970s — but never written as a doubling recursion for the integrated-observable second moment. The most recent and most spiritually aligned paper, **Münch et al. (2022, eLife)**, explicitly identifies the generalized-multinomial dynamics on the symmetric subspace and the Gaussian/Kalman closure as an approximation, and explicitly notes that exact alternatives "avoid the normal approximation" but do not implement them — which is precisely the gap the proposed algorithm fills.

- Colquhoun & Hawkes (1981) "On the stochastic properties of single ion channels," *Proc. R. Soc. B* 211:205–235. DOI 10.1098/rspb.1981.0003. **Partially overlaps** — single-particle Q-matrix/expm foundation only.
- Colquhoun & Hawkes (1982) "Bursts of single ion channel openings," *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* 300:1–59. DOI 10.1098/rstb.1982.0156. **Partially overlaps** — the canonical reference for single-channel mean and variance of integrated functionals (total open time per burst); no N-particle composition.
- Colquhoun & Hawkes (1990) "Two-channel patch," *Proc. R. Soc. B* 240:453–477. DOI 10.1098/rspb.1990.0048. **Partially overlaps** — closest classical N>1 prior art; explicit construction at N=2, no doubling recursion.
- Milescu, Akk & Sachs (2005) "Maximum Likelihood Estimation of Ion Channel Kinetics from Macroscopic Currents," *Biophys. J.* 88:2494–2515. DOI 10.1529/biophysj.104.053256. **Partially overlaps** — analytical gradients of the macroscopic Gaussian/multinomial likelihood; no Kronecker doubling, no σ² of integrated observable, no autodiff.
- Moffatt (2007) "Estimation of Ion Channel Kinetics from Fluctuations of Macroscopic Currents," *Biophys. J.* 93:74–91. DOI 10.1529/biophysj.106.101212. **Partially overlaps** — Kalman filter on generalized-multinomial mean+covariance, finite-difference gradients.
- Stepanyuk, Borisyuk & Belan (2011) PLoS ONE 6:e29731. DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0029731. **Clearly distinct** — analytical gradient via covariance semi-separability; eigendecomposition of single-channel Q (the very thing the algorithm avoids).
- Münch, Paul, Schmauder, Benndorf & Goldschen-Ohm (2022) "Bayesian inference of kinetic schemes for ion channels by Kalman filtering," *eLife* 11:e62714. DOI 10.7554/eLife.62714. **Partially overlaps — strongest direct conceptual neighbor.**
- Del Core & Mirams (2025) "Parameter inference for stochastic reaction models of ion channel gating," *Phil. Trans. A* 383:20240224. DOI 10.1098/rsta.2024.0224. **Partially overlaps** — sensitivity-ODEs through differential moment equations, not exact propagator.
- Jaxley (Deistler et al. 2025) *Nat. Methods*, DOI 10.1038/s41592-025-02895-w. **Clearly distinct** — autodiff through deterministic rate ODE only.
- Siekmann, Sneyd & Crampin (2011, 2012) MCMC for ion channels, *Biophys. J.* DOI 10.1016/j.bpj.2011.02.059. **Clearly distinct** — gradient-free, single-channel.

## 2. Stochastic chemical kinetics / chemical master equation

The CME literature contains the **algebraic** ancestor of the proposed construction: **Jahnke & Huisinga (2007)** prove that the time-dependent solution of a monomolecular (= non-interacting) CME is a convolution of multinomial and product Poisson distributions with parameters evolving by the deterministic rate equation. This is the same Kronecker-sum / multinomial-convolution structure — derived probabilistically rather than as a numerical recipe. Vastola (2021) re-derives it via Doi–Peliti coherent-state path integrals and makes the bosonic Fock-space connection explicit. None of these works provides the boundary-conditioned **(m, μ, σ²)** bundle, none packages the construction as a binary-doubling propagator algorithm, and none targets differentiable inference. The 2024–2026 differentiable-CME line (Differentiable Gillespie of Moen & Mehta; Gumbel-Softmax SSA; DeepCME; Sukys et al. Nessie; Lück & Wolf 2023) is overwhelmingly **trajectory-based** rather than propagator-based, confirming that the proposed algorithm occupies a distinct algorithmic niche.

- Jahnke & Huisinga (2007) "Solving the chemical master equation for monomolecular reaction systems analytically," *J. Math. Biol.* 54:1–26. DOI 10.1007/s00285-006-0034-x. **Closest classical algebraic ancestor; partially overlaps** — gives the multinomial-convolution identity but only for marginal distribution, not the (m, μ, σ²) bundle, no doubling recursion, no autodiff.
- Vastola (2021) "Solving the CME for monomolecular reaction systems and beyond: a Doi–Peliti path integral view," *J. Math. Biol.* 84:21. DOI 10.1007/s00285-021-01670-7. **Partially overlaps** — Doi–Peliti rederivation, names the bosonic Fock-space connection.
- Reis, Kromer & Klipp (2018) "Hierarchic first-order reaction networks," *J. Math. Biol.* 77:377–419. DOI 10.1007/s00285-018-1205-2. **Clearly distinct** — extends Jahnke to hierarchic first-order networks, still marginal distribution only.
- Munsky & Khammash (2006) Finite State Projection, *J. Chem. Phys.* 124:044104. DOI 10.1063/1.2145882. **Clearly distinct** — generic state-space truncation, no independence factorization.
- Fox, Neuert & Munsky (2019) "FSP-FIM," *PLOS Comp. Biol.* 15:e1006365. DOI 10.1371/journal.pcbi.1006365. **Clearly distinct** — sensitivity-ODE Fisher info on top of FSP, not Kronecker-factored.
- Moen & Mehta (2024–25) "Differentiable Gillespie Algorithm," arXiv:2407.04865. **Clearly distinct** — smooths Gillespie's discrete operations with sigmoids; trajectory-level approximation.
- Sukys, Sahoo, Öcal, Grima & Sanguinetti (2022) "Nessie," *PLOS Comp. Biol.* 18:e1010492. DOI 10.1371/journal.pcbi.1010492. **Clearly distinct** — NN surrogate for marginal CME.
- Gorin, Vastola & Pachter (2023) "Studying stochastic systems biology of the cell with single-cell genomics data," *Cell Systems*. DOI 10.1016/j.cels.2023.08.004. **Partially overlaps** — generating-function/PGF-based monomolecular CME inference; no (μ, σ²) bundle or doubling.

## 3. Tensor decompositions for CME / CTMC ensembles

Across the TT/QTT/HT and tree-tensor-network CME literature, every method (Kazeev–Khammash–Nip–Schwab 2014; Dolgov–Khoromskij 2015; Vo–Sidje 2017; Einkemmer–Mangott–Prugger 2024; Ion et al. 2021; Dolgov–Savostyanov 2024) uses **distinguishable-species product indexing** — one tensor mode per species or per labeled individual — and computes either the marginal probability vector p(t) or the stationary distribution via ALS/AMEn/DMRG fitting or projector-splitting dynamical low-rank integrators. None exploits the **identical-particle symmetry** that collapses S^N to the multinomial subspace of dimension C(N+S−1, S−1), none builds the propagator by direct algebraic composition of half-systems (binary doubling), and none produces the boundary-conditioned (m, μ, σ²) bundle. The closest inference work, **Ion et al. (2021)**, embeds parameters as additional TT modes and does forward–backward filtering, but inference is via ALS on TT cores plus MCMC, not autodiff through a `+/×` propagator.

## 4. Identical-particle propagators in physics and quantum many-body

The construction **single-particle propagator → many-body symmetric-subspace propagator via products of single-particle propagators** is textbook folklore in second quantization for non-interacting bosons (Negele–Orland; Fetter–Walecka; Dickhoff–Van Neck). The classical-stochastic import is the **Doi (1976) / Peliti (1985) formalism**, with Jahnke & Huisinga (2007) supplying the probabilistic version and Vastola (2021) the path-integral version. The open-quantum analog is **Prosen's third quantization** for quadratic (non-interacting) Lindbladians, where the Liouvillian decouples on single-particle modes. Crucially, **no source imports this folklore as an inference algorithm with the boundary-conditioned (m, μ, σ²) bundle of an integrated classical observable**: the aggregate-HMM / Collective Graphical Model line of Sheldon and collaborators addresses the *exact* problem statement (N i.i.d. individuals running the same HMM, observed only in aggregate counts) but uses Markov-basis MCMC, Sinkhorn belief propagation, or Gaussian limits, **explicitly missing the bosonic Fock-space lift**.

- Doi (1976) "Second quantization for classical many-particle systems," *J. Phys. A* 9:1465. DOI 10.1088/0305-4470/9/9/008. **Folklore — partially overlaps** — formal substrate.
- Peliti (1985) "Path integral approach to birth-death processes on a lattice," *J. Physique* 46:1469. DOI 10.1051/jphys:019850046090146900. **Folklore.**
- Negele & Orland (1988) *Quantum Many-Particle Systems*, Westview. **Folklore reference** — non-interacting many-body propagator = symmetrized product of single-particle propagators.
- Prosen (2008) "Third quantization for quadratic open Fermi systems," *New J. Phys.* 10:043026. DOI 10.1088/1367-2630/10/4/043026. **Strong conceptual overlap** — exact open-quantum analog of Q_total = Q ⊕ … ⊕ Q for non-interacting Lindbladians; no integrated-observable bundle, no classical-CTMC inference.
- Barthel & Zhang (2022) "Solving quasi-free and quadratic Lindblad master equations," *J. Stat. Mech.* 113101. DOI 10.1088/1742-5468/ac8e5c. **Partially overlaps** — closed-form propagator for non-interacting Lindbladians.
- McGinley et al. (2024) "Linear and Non-Linear Response of Quadratic Lindbladians," *npj Quantum Materials*. DOI 10.1038/s41535-024-00709-4. **Closest analog of μ_ij as Green's function** for non-interacting open quantum systems.
- Sheldon & Dietterich (2011) "Collective Graphical Models," NeurIPS. **Partially overlaps on problem framing**, distinct on method — Markov-basis MCMC, no Fock-space lift.
- Singh, Zhang & Chen (2021) "Learning HMMs from Aggregate Observations," *Automatica*. DOI 10.1016/j.automatica.2021.110078. **Partially overlaps**, Gaussian/Sinkhorn approximations.
- Strand et al. (2023) "Quantifying Rare Events with Tensor Networks," *Phys. Rev. X* 13:041006. DOI 10.1103/PhysRevX.13.041006. **Partially overlaps** — Doi–Peliti MPO + TDVP; rare-event focus, not inference bundle.

## 5. Filtering and Bayesian inference for compositional / multinomial-state HMMs

This is where the most direct competitor lives. **Ho, Crawford & Suchard (2018, AOAS)** explicitly target exact differentiable transition probabilities for stochastic SIR enabling HMC — the very same selling point — but build them from continued-fraction / Laplace-transform recursions on the full bivariate count space, **not** from Kronecker-sum factorization across N particles, and they handle only m_ij with no μ or σ² of integrated observables. **Sherlock (2021)** argues for direct Krylov expm-based inference on finite-state MJPs (no autodiff). The approximate-filtering family (Whiteley–Rimella 2021; Whitehouse–Whiteley–Rimella PAL 2023; Whitehouse 2025 LawPAL embedded in Stan) addresses the same setting but replaces the exact propagator with multinomial/Poisson assumed-density approximations. **FactorialHMM (Schweiger, Erlich & Carmi 2019)** exploits exactly the Cartesian-product / independence factorization the proposal generalizes — but in discrete time, for non-identical chains, with no integrated-observable moments and no autodiff. **King, Lin & Ionides (2025)** treat exchangeable-individual compartment models via genealogy filter equations evaluated by Monte Carlo. None of these combines population CTMC + Kronecker-sum/binary-doubling + boundary-conditioned σ² + autodiff.

- Ho, Crawford & Suchard (2018) "Direct likelihood-based inference for stochastic compartmental models," *Annals of Applied Statistics* 12:1993–2021. DOI 10.1214/18-AOAS1141. **Closest direct competitor** — partially overlaps on goal, clearly distinct on method (continued fractions, no Kronecker, m_ij only).
- Sherlock (2021) "Direct statistical inference for finite Markov jump processes via the matrix exponential," *Comput. Stat.* 36:2361–2387. DOI 10.1007/s00180-021-01102-6. **Partially overlaps** — direct expm + Krylov, no autodiff.
- Whiteley & Rimella (2021) "Inference in stochastic epidemic models via multinomial approximations," AISTATS. arXiv:2006.13700. **Partially overlaps** — multinomial state representation, but approximate.
- Whitehouse, Whiteley & Rimella (2023) "Poisson Approximate Likelihoods," *J. R. Stat. Soc. B* 85:1173. DOI 10.1093/jrsssb/qkad065. **Partially overlaps** — assumed-density approximation.
- Whitehouse (2025) LawPAL, arXiv:2505.06935. **Partially overlaps** — Stan/HMC autodiff for *approximate* compartmental likelihoods.
- Schweiger, Erlich & Carmi (2019) "FactorialHMM," *Bioinformatics* 35:2162–2164. DOI 10.1093/bioinformatics/bty944. **Partially overlaps on structural insight** — discrete-time, no autodiff, no integrated observables.
- Tan, Hooker & Ionides (2024) "Accelerated inference for partially observed Markov processes using AD," arXiv:2407.03085. **Partially overlaps** — AD-friendly particle filters, Monte Carlo, not exact-Kronecker.
- Komorowski et al. (2009) "LNA Bayesian inference," *BMC Bioinformatics* 10:343. DOI 10.1186/1471-2105-10-343. **Clearly distinct** — Gaussian moment closure.

## 6. Differentiable matrix exponential and alternatives

The single closest paper to the proposed algorithm across all six fields is **Rupp, Schill, Süskind, Lange et al. (2024) "Differentiated uniformization,"** which targets exactly the same problem class — CTMC ensembles whose Q is a tensor sum on combinatorial state spaces, where ∂exp(tQ)/∂θ is needed for HMC, demonstrated on stochastic SIR. The mechanism differs (uniformization power series vs. binary doubling) and the output is smaller (m_ij and its derivative only — no μ_ij or σ²_ij conditional moments). The numerical-analysis state of the art for Fréchet-derivative-of-expm is **Al-Mohy & Higham (2009)**, which is what `scipy.linalg.expm_frechet` and `jax.scipy.linalg.expm_frechet` implement; it sidesteps eigendecomposition and is therefore degeneracy-robust, but requires the full M×M matrix. The phylogenetics community (Ji et al. 2020; Gangavarapu et al. 2024 BEAGLE; Magee et al. 2024; Didier et al. 2024 PNAS; PhyloGrad 2025) is the most prominent autodiff-MLE/HMC consumer of exp(QΔt), and explicitly documents the **degenerate-eigenvalue / non-diagonalizable failure mode** (Schranz et al. 2008 "Pathological rate matrices") — confirming the proposal's stated pain point. The **σ²_ij centered-variance object as part of a routine differentiable bundle does not appear in any source surveyed**; Carbonell, Jiménez & Biscay (2008) generalize Van Loan's block-triangular trick to multiple integrals, which would suffice for σ², but it is not packaged with autodiff or doubling.

- Rupp, Schill, Süskind, Lange et al. (2024) "Differentiated uniformization," *Comput. Stat.* 39:3643–3663. DOI 10.1007/s00180-024-01454-9 (arXiv:2112.10971). **Closest prior art across all six fields.** Same problem class, different mechanism, smaller bundle.
- Al-Mohy & Higham (2009) "Computing the Fréchet derivative of the matrix exponential," *SIAM J. Matrix Anal. Appl.* 30:1639–1657. DOI 10.1137/080716426. **Partially overlaps** — workhorse Fréchet derivative; full M×M cost.
- Al-Mohy & Higham (2011) "Computing the action of the matrix exponential," *SIAM J. Sci. Comput.* 33:488–511. DOI 10.1137/100788860. **Clearly distinct** — expmv, no derivative.
- Van Loan (1978) "Computing integrals involving the matrix exponential," *IEEE Trans. Autom. Control* 23:395–404. **Partially overlaps** — block-triangular trick underlies μ_ij computation at single-CTMC level.
- Carbonell, Jiménez & Biscay (2008) "Computing multiple integrals involving matrix exponentials," *J. Comput. Appl. Math.* 213:300–305. DOI 10.1016/j.cam.2007.01.007. **Partially overlaps** — multiple integrals, would suffice for σ² at single-CTMC level.
- Hobolth & Jensen (2011) "Summary statistics for endpoint-conditioned CTMCs," *J. Appl. Probab.* 48:911–924. DOI 10.1239/jap/1324046009. **Partially overlaps** — gives μ_ij at single-CTMC level via EVD/uniformization.
- Tataru & Hobolth (2011) "Comparison of methods for endpoint-conditioned sufficient statistics," *BMC Bioinformatics* 12:465. DOI 10.1186/1471-2105-12-465. **Partially overlaps** — three single-CTMC methods for μ_ij.
- Minin & Suchard (2008) "Counting labeled transitions," *J. Math. Biol.* 56:391–412. DOI 10.1007/s00285-007-0120-8. **Partially overlaps** — augmented-generator trick for second-moment objects, single CTMC.
- Schranz et al. (2008) "Pathological rate matrices," *BMC Bioinformatics*. PMC2639438. **Partially overlaps** — diagnoses the degenerate-eigenvalue failure mode the algorithm avoids.
- Didier, Glatt-Holtz, Holbrook, Magee & Suchard (2024) PNAS 121:e2318989121. DOI 10.1073/pnas.2318989121. **Highly relevant** — first-order ∂exp(tQ)/∂Q ≈ tI approximation for SARS-CoV-2 phylogeography HMC; approximate, not exact, no bundle.
- Ji, Suchard, Lemey et al. (2020) "Gradients do grow on trees," *Mol. Biol. Evol.* 37:3047. **Partially overlaps** — analytic chain rule on tree avoids autodiff through expm; branch-length focus.
- Gangavarapu et al. (2024) BEAGLE many-core gradients, *Bioinformatics* 40:btae030. DOI 10.1093/bioinformatics/btae030. **Clearly distinct.**
- Krämer, Moreno-Muñoz, Roy & Hauberg (2024) "Gradients of Functions of Large Matrices," NeurIPS, arXiv:2405.17277. **Partially overlaps** — adjoint Lanczos/Arnoldi for matrix-function gradients without forming the full matrix; Krylov route.

## Synthesis

**The published work that comes closest is Rupp, Schill, Süskind, Lange et al. (2024) "Differentiated uniformization" (DOI 10.1007/s00180-024-01454-9)**, because it solves the same inference problem class — gradient-based inference of CTMC ensembles whose generator is a tensor sum on a combinatorial (population-style) state space, applied to stochastic SIR — and explicitly delivers ∂exp(tQ)/∂θ without ever forming the full M×M matrix. The mechanism is uniformization rather than binary doubling, and it produces only the transition-probability derivative (m_ij and ∂m_ij/∂θ); it does not carry the conditional-mean μ_ij and conditional-variance σ²_ij of an integrated observable, and it does not exploit identical-particle / multinomial-symmetric-subspace structure (its tensor-sum representation is for distinguishable species). The next-closest neighbors are **Ho, Crawford & Suchard (2018, AOAS)** — same HMC-on-stochastic-SIR-via-differentiable-transition-probabilities goal, but continued fractions on full bivariate counts, not Kronecker doubling; **Münch et al. (2022, eLife)** — explicitly identifies the symmetric-subspace generalized-multinomial process for N-channel patches and the Gaussian/Kalman closure as an approximation, without implementing the exact alternative; **Jahnke & Huisinga (2007)** plus **Vastola (2021)** — the multinomial-convolution algebra of monomolecular CMEs, in probabilistic and Doi–Peliti language respectively, but for marginal distributions, no boundary conditioning, no autodiff; and **Hobolth–Jensen (2011) / Tataru–Hobolth (2011) / Minin–Suchard (2008)** — endpoint-conditioned moments of integrated functionals at the single-CTMC level (μ_ij rigorously; σ²_ij via Minin–Suchard's augmented-generator trick).

**The genuine gap**, defensibly novel from this search, is the *combination* of (i) the symmetric-subspace propagator built by **binary doubling on N with multinomial convolution** (rather than uniformization, ALS/AMEn fitting, or full M×M expm), (ii) the **boundary-conditioned (m, μ, σ²) bundle** carried through the recursion with linear-additive composition rules — in particular the explicit use of variance additivity from conditional independence given split-endpoints to motivate tracking **centered** rather than raw second moments — and (iii) **forward-mode autodiff** through a `+/×`-only computation, sidestepping both eigendecomposition-based instability (Schranz 2008's pathological rate matrices) and the cost of full-M×M Al-Mohy–Higham Fréchet derivatives, enabling exact-microscopic gradient-based MLE / Fisher information / HMC at experimental N where Gaussian moment closure (Milescu 2005; Moffatt 2007; Münch 2022; LNA family) breaks down.

**The strongest defensible novelty claim**: *"The first algorithm to compute the exact boundary-conditioned moment bundle (transition probability, conditional mean, and conditional variance of an integrated observable) for an ensemble of N independent identical CTMCs by binary doubling on N — exploiting Kronecker-sum factorization on the multinomial symmetric subspace — in a form that is trivially differentiable by forward-mode autodiff, enabling gradient-based MLE, Fisher-information, and HMC inference at the microscopic level without forming or eigendecomposing the M×M ensemble generator."* This claim is robust to the prior art surveyed: each ingredient has precedent, but no published work combines all three.

**Honest folklore caveats.** (a) The Kronecker-sum identity exp(Q⊕Q) = P⊗P, and its consequence that non-interacting many-body propagators factor into single-particle propagators on the symmetric subspace, is **textbook folklore in second quantization** (Negele–Orland; Fetter–Walecka) and has been re-derived multiple times in classical-stochastic guise (Doi 1976; Peliti 1985; Jahnke & Huisinga 2007; Vastola 2021; Prosen 2008 for quadratic Lindbladians). The proposed algorithm should not claim novelty for this identity itself. (b) The endpoint-conditioned (μ_ij, σ²_ij) of an integrated functional is well-developed at single-CTMC level (Hobolth, Tataru, Minin, Suchard) and the σ² extension via Carbonell et al. 2008 multiple-integral block-triangular augmentation is straightforward — the proposed algorithm is best framed as **lifting these single-CTMC primitives to the population level via independence, with the variance-additivity-given-split-endpoints observation enabling the σ² bundle to compose linearly through the doubling recursion**. (c) The contribution is therefore best positioned as an *algorithmic synthesis and a differentiable-inference primitive*, not a fundamentally new mathematical identity. The novelty is real but it lives in the integration, not in any single piece.
